---
title: French Onion Soup
category: Soups
serves: 4
time: 1 hour 45 minutes
---

The kind of soup that makes the whole house smell good for hours. It only works
if you take your time with the onions — twenty minutes with the lid on, then
another fifteen uncovered until they're genuinely dark and sweet. Don't rush
that part.

## Ingredients

- 8 large onions, halved and finely sliced
- 60 g butter
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 4 cloves garlic, finely sliced
- 1.5 litres beef stock
- 185 ml dry white wine, dry vermouth, port, or cognac (for the deglaze)
- 125 ml port or cognac (to finish)
- 4–5 sprigs [thyme](/produce/02-herbs/09-thyme/)
- 1–2 [bay leaves](/produce/02-herbs/02-bay/)
- ½ tsp nutmeg
- Salt and pepper

## Method

1. Heat the butter and olive oil together in a large, heavy pan over medium-low
   heat.
2. Tip in the onions and stir to coat. Put the lid on and cook for 20 minutes,
   stirring occasionally, until softened and collapsed.
3. Remove the lid, increase the heat to medium, and add ½ tsp salt. Cook for
   a further 15 minutes, stirring regularly, until the onions are a deep golden
   brown. Don't rush this — the colour is the flavour.
4. Add the garlic, thyme, bay, and nutmeg. Stir and cook for 2 minutes.
5. Deglaze with your chosen liquid — white wine, vermouth, port, or cognac —
   scraping up anything stuck to the base. Let it reduce by half.
6. Pour in the stock, bring to a gentle simmer, and cook uncovered for
   45 minutes.
7. Stir through the port or cognac, taste, and adjust the seasoning. Fish out
   the thyme sprigs and bay leaves before serving.
