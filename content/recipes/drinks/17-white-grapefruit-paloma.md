---
title: White Grapefruit Paloma
serves: 1
time: 5 minutes
---

The Paloma is one of those drinks that reveals how good a piece of fruit
actually is. Our [white grapefruit](/produce/01-fruit/03-white-grapefruit/)
is bitter and assertive enough to hold its own against the tequila rather than
disappearing behind it — sweeter commercial grapefruit juice makes a flatter
drink. Squeeze it fresh; the flavour fades quickly.

## Ingredients

- 60 ml blanco tequila
- 90 ml fresh [white grapefruit](/produce/01-fruit/03-white-grapefruit/) juice
  (about half a large grapefruit)
- 15 ml fresh lime juice
- 10 ml agave syrup
- 60–80 ml soda water, to top
- Flaked salt
- Ice
- Grapefruit wedge to garnish

## Method

1. Run a grapefruit wedge around the rim of a tall glass and press it into
   a small plate of flaked salt to coat the rim. Fill the glass with ice.
2. Combine the tequila, grapefruit juice, lime juice, and agave syrup in
   a shaker with ice. Shake briefly — just 5–6 seconds to chill and combine.
3. Strain into the prepared glass over the ice.
4. Top with soda water and stir gently once.
5. Squeeze the grapefruit wedge over the top and drop it in.

## Substitutions

**[White grapefruit](/produce/01-fruit/03-white-grapefruit/)** — Ruby Red
or pink grapefruit gives a sweeter, less bitter drink; reduce the agave
to 5 ml to compensate. Cara Cara orange is completely different but
equally good in a sour direction.

**Agave syrup** — Simple syrup works fine; agave has a slightly softer,
less sugary sweetness that suits the drink.

**Tequila** — A reposado adds a woody note that works well with the
bitterness. Mezcal takes it smokier.
