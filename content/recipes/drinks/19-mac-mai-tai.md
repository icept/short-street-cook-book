---
title: The Mac Mai Tai
serves: 1
time: 5 minutes
---

Trader Vic's 1944 original called for almond orgeat, aged Jamaican rum, and
fresh lime. The macadamia version keeps all three ideas and improves on the
third — our [orgeat](/recipes/syrups-cordials/05-rich-toasted-macadamia-orgeat/)
is richer and more complex than most commercial almond syrups, so the drink
doesn't need to be sweetened up to work. Shake it properly; a sloppy Mai Tai
is a sad thing.

## Ingredients

- 45 ml aged Jamaican rum
- 15 ml white or agricole rum
- 20 ml [Rich Toasted Macadamia Orgeat](/recipes/syrups-cordials/05-rich-toasted-macadamia-orgeat/)
- 15 ml dry orange curaçao (Cointreau or Pierre Ferrand)
- 30 ml fresh lime juice
- Crushed ice
- Mint sprig and lime wheel to garnish

## Method

1. Combine the aged rum, white rum, orgeat, curaçao, and lime juice in a shaker
   with regular ice.
2. Shake hard for 10–12 seconds.
3. Strain over a glass packed with crushed ice.
4. Garnish with a mint sprig pressed firmly between your palms first to wake
   up the oils, and a lime wheel on the rim.

## Flavour

Citrus-forward with a long, buttery finish from the macadamia orgeat. The two
rums sit behind the lime rather than in front of it — present and warm but
not dominant. Drier and more complex than the syrupy tourist versions.

## Why it works

Two rums rather than one gives the drink more dimension — the agricole or white
rum adds a grassy, funky top note that the aged rum alone doesn't have. The
orgeat does double duty as both sweetener and flavouring; because the macadamia
version is already complex, you need less of it than you would with a plain
simple syrup.

## Substitutions

**[Macadamia orgeat](/recipes/syrups-cordials/05-rich-toasted-macadamia-orgeat/)** — Almond orgeat is the
original and works straight up; the flavour is cleaner and less rich.

**White rum** — Omit and use 60 ml aged rum if you only have one bottle.
A float of dark rum over the top in place of the white is also good.

**Dry orange curaçao** — Triple sec works; it's sweeter, so reduce the
orgeat to 15 ml to keep the balance.
