---
title: Cara Cara Mac Sour
category: Drinks
serves: 1
time: 5 minutes
---

A cognac sour built around our own macadamia orgeat and a Cara Cara orange off
the tree. The Cara Cara is sweeter and rounder than a navel, with a faint berry
note, so the lime does the sharpening — a lemon version comes out flat.

## Ingredients

- 60 ml cognac (VS or VSOP is plenty)
- 40 ml fresh Cara Cara orange juice
- 25 ml fresh lime juice
- 20 ml macadamia orgeat (home-made)
- 1 dash Angostura bitters
- Ice

## Method

1. Add the cognac, Cara Cara juice, lime juice, orgeat, and bitters to a shaker.
2. Fill with ice and shake hard for 10–15 seconds, until the shaker frosts.
3. Strain over fresh ice into a rocks glass, or strain up into a chilled coupe
   if you want it sharper and colder.
4. No garnish needed, but a strip of Cara Cara peel over the top is nice.

## Flavour

Buttery and nutty underneath from the macadamia orgeat, with the Cara Cara
sitting sweet and slightly floral against the lime's sharpness. The cognac
warms the whole thing and the bitters keep it from going soft.

## Why it works

Cara Cara is low in acid, so the lime carries the sour duty rather than the
orange — pairing it with lemon instead would read flat and one-note. The orgeat
isn't just sweetener: its richness gives the drink body, so it lands creamy
rather than thin. Shake hard, as the dilution and chill are what pull the
spirit, sweet, and sour into balance.

## Substitutions

**Cara Cara** — A regular navel orange works but loses the berry note; cut the
lime to 20 ml to compensate for the higher acidity. Blood orange is excellent
when they're around.

**Cognac** — A decent brandy stands in fine. Aged rum takes it in a more
tropical, tiki direction.

**Macadamia orgeat** — Almond orgeat is the classic and works straight up,
just a touch less buttery.
