---
title: Cognac & Cara Cara Roast Chicken
serves: 4
time: 1 hour 30 minutes (plus 8–12 hours to marinate)
---

This one takes planning but rewards you well. The [Cara Cara](/produce/01-fruit/02-cara-cara-orange/) oranges caramelise
around the bird as it roasts, and the cognac marinade reduces to a dark, glossy
syrup that you pour over the carved meat at the table. Use the conventional
oven setting — fan-forced will scorch the sugar before the chicken is done.

## Ingredients

### The bird and aromatics
- 1 whole chicken, about 2 kg
- 2 [Cara Cara navel oranges](/produce/01-fruit/02-cara-cara-orange/) — peel one completely, leave the other unpeeled;
  slice both into 5 mm rounds
- 2 large onions, cut into thick wedges (keep the root on so they hold together)
- Large handful fresh [thyme](/produce/02-herbs/09-thyme/) sprigs

### The marinade
- 100 ml cognac
- 4 tbsp olive oil
- 3 tbsp fresh orange juice
- 3 tbsp fresh [lemon](/produce/01-fruit/01-lemon/) juice
- 3 tbsp light brown sugar
- 1½ tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1½ tsp fennel seeds, lightly toasted and crushed
- 2½ tsp fine salt
- 1½ tsp black pepper

## Method

1. Whisk together all the marinade ingredients until the sugar has mostly
   dissolved.
2. Sit the chicken on a rimmed board. Spoon a few tablespoons of marinade into
   the cavity along with 2–3 onion wedges and a thyme sprig.
3. Put the chicken, sliced oranges, remaining onion wedges, thyme, and
   remaining marinade into a large vacuum bag. To seal without losing the
   liquid, hang the bag over the counter edge so everything pools at the
   bottom, then pull the vacuum from the top and seal just before the liquid
   reaches the machine. Refrigerate for 8–12 hours.
4. Remove the chicken from the fridge while you preheat the oven to 190°C
   (conventional, not fan — the sugar will scorch on fan-forced).
5. Cut open the bag and tip the entire contents into a high-sided roasting tin.
   Sit the chicken breast-side up and spread the oranges, onions, and herbs
   around it.
6. Roast for 1 hour 30 minutes, basting with the pan juices every 20 minutes.
   In the final 30 minutes, keep an eye on the skin — if the sugars are
   darkening too fast, lay a loose sheet of aluminium foil over the tin.
7. The chicken is done when the thigh reads 74°C at its thickest point. Lift
   the bird, caramelised onions, and oranges onto a carving board and rest for
   15 minutes.
8. While it rests, pour the pan liquid into a small saucepan and simmer over
   medium-high heat for 5–10 minutes until thick and glossy.
9. Carve the chicken and plate alongside the onions and roasted oranges. Pour
   the hot cognac syrup over the meat just before serving.
