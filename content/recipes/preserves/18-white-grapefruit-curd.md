---
title: White Grapefruit Curd
yield: about 500 ml
time: 25 minutes, plus cooling
---

The bitterness of the [white grapefruit](/produce/01-fruit/03-white-grapefruit/)
doesn't disappear in the curd — it mellows into something more interesting than
plain lemon curd, with a slight edge behind the sweetness that keeps it from
being cloying. Use it on toast, in a tart case, between sponge layers, or
spooned alongside yoghurt and something crunchy.

## Ingredients

- 180 ml fresh [white grapefruit](/produce/01-fruit/03-white-grapefruit/) juice
  (2–3 grapefruits, depending on size)
- Finely grated zest of 1 [white grapefruit](/produce/01-fruit/03-white-grapefruit/)
- 160 g caster sugar
- 3 large eggs plus 2 egg yolks
- 120 g cold unsalted butter, cut into small cubes

## Method

1. Combine the grapefruit juice, zest, sugar, whole eggs, and yolks in a
   heatproof bowl. Whisk until smooth.
2. Set the bowl over a saucepan of barely simmering water — the bottom of
   the bowl should not touch the water. Stir constantly with a silicone
   spatula or wooden spoon, scraping the sides and base of the bowl.
3. Cook for 12–15 minutes until the mixture has thickened noticeably — it
   should coat the back of the spatula and hold a line drawn through it.
   Don't rush the heat; scrambled eggs are not the goal.
4. Remove from the heat. Add the cold butter a few cubes at a time, stirring
   each addition through fully before adding the next. The cold butter cools
   the curd as it enriches it.
5. Strain through a fine sieve into a jug, pressing through with the spatula
   to remove the zest. Pour into sterilised jars and seal.
6. Refrigerate once cooled. The curd sets further as it chills.

## Notes

**Shelf life:** keeps in the fridge for two to three weeks. The high egg
content means it doesn't keep as long as jams or preserved lemons —
make a batch when the grapefruit is in season and use it steadily.

**Setting point:** if the curd seems loose after chilling, it's usually
fine — it continues to thicken for several hours. A truly loose curd
that doesn't set overnight was probably undercooked; it's still usable
as a sauce or a drink mixer.

**Tart filling:** pour the warm curd directly into a blind-baked
shortcrust or sweet pastry case and refrigerate until set. A little
softly whipped cream alongside is all it needs.
