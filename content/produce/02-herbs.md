The herb patch is the most-used part of the garden. Hardy Mediterranean herbs
grow themselves with almost no help here in Perth — the rosemary is nearly a
small hedge — while the soft annual herbs need a bit more thought about where
to plant them and when.

## In season

Rosemary, thyme, oregano, bay, and sage are year-round, though they're most
vigorous and full-flavoured during the warmer months. Basil needs warmth —
plant out after the last cool snap, harvest freely from November through to
March before the nights cool again. Coriander and parsley prefer the cooler
months; both bolt quickly in Perth's summer heat, so autumn and winter are
their season here.

## Pairs with

- Garlic — goes with almost every herb in the patch
- Lemon — especially parsley, coriander, oregano, thyme
- Olive oil — herb oils, dressings, finishing drizzles
- Chilli — coriander, basil, and Thai basil especially
- Anchovy — parsley, thyme, rosemary in braises and butters
- Nuts — parsley and walnut, basil and pine nut
- Yoghurt — mint, coriander, dill (when we grow it)

## Where to get it locally

- Garden, always first
- Seedlings and cut bunches at **Stirling Farmers Market** (Sundays)
- Dried herbs in bulk at **Kakulas Bros** (Northbridge) — good range,
  well-priced, turns over fast so the stock is fresh

## Notes

**Salsa verde:** the best use of a glut. Parsley, capers, anchovies, garlic,
olive oil, a little vinegar — keeps in the fridge for a week under a layer
of oil.

**Herb oils:** blanch soft herbs briefly, squeeze dry, blend with olive oil,
strain. Freeze in ice-cube trays. Basil oil and parsley oil are both
excellent.

**Drying woody herbs:** tie rosemary, thyme, and oregano in small bundles and
hang in a dry spot. Strip and jar when brittle. The dried versions work well in
long-cooked dishes where the fresh would dominate.

**Bay leaves** from your own tree are a different thing to the dried ones from
the supermarket — use them fresh whenever possible.
