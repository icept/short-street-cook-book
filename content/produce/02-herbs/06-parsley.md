Flat-leaf parsley is the one to grow — curly is decorative but flat-leaf
has actual flavour. It's the most versatile herb in the patch: equally good
as the main event (a salsa verde, a gremolata) or as background texture.
The stems have as much flavour as the leaves and belong in stocks and braises.

## In season

April through October. Bolts in Perth's summer heat, so autumn planting
gives the longest season. A winter sowing extends into spring before
the heat finishes it. Keep it cut to delay bolting.

## Pairs with

- Garlic — in everything from gremolata to persillade
- Anchovy — parsley in a bagna cauda, in a butter for steak
- Lemon — in a gremolata, a dressing, a chimichurri
- Capers — in a salsa verde or tartare sauce
- Walnut — parsley and walnut in a pasta or spread
- Butter — simple parsley butter, good on fish and steak
- Chickpeas and lentils — fresh parsley lifts a bowl of cooked legumes

## Where to get it locally

- Own patch in cooler months
- Year-round bunches at most supermarkets and Asian grocers;
  look for flat-leaf, not curly
- **Stirling Farmers Market** (Sundays) in season

## Notes

**Salsa verde:** the best use of a glut. Parsley, capers, anchovies, garlic,
olive oil, a splash of red wine vinegar — blitz or chop. Keeps in the fridge
under a layer of oil for up to a week.

**Parsley oil:** blanch briefly, squeeze dry, blend with neutral oil, strain.
Freeze in ice-cube trays. Bright green and useful.

**Use the stems:** chop fine and add to stocks, braises, or anything cooking
low and slow. Don't throw them away.
