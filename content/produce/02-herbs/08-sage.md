Sage is more assertive than most of the other herbs in the patch — musky,
slightly bitter, and needs something rich to work alongside. Butter is its
best partner. Fried in brown butter for a few seconds it becomes crisp and
nutty; used fresh in too large a quantity it can overpower everything around it.
A little goes a long way.

## In season

Year-round. An undemanding plant in Perth's climate; grows in a dry, sunny
spot with minimal water. The flavour is fullest in summer.

## Pairs with

- Brown butter — the definitive pairing; sage crisped in brown butter
  is a sauce for pasta, gnocchi, pumpkin, and fish
- Pork — in stuffings, sausages, and slow-cooked shoulder
- Pumpkin — roasted or in a soup with sage butter
- Chicken — under the skin or in the cavity with garlic
- Dried beans — especially borlotti and cannellini in a braise
- Apple — in a pork stuffing or sauce
- Aged cheese — fried sage alongside shaved pecorino or parmesan

## Where to get it locally

- Own plant
- Potted sage is widely available at nurseries and supermarket herb sections;
  easy to establish

## Notes

**Crispy fried sage:** heat a good knob of butter in a small pan until it
foams and starts to brown. Drop in whole sage leaves and fry for 20–30 seconds
until crisp. Lift out onto paper and use as a garnish — they stay crisp for
an hour or two.

**Use sparingly raw:** fresh sage in salads or cold applications can be
overpowering. It works better with heat.
