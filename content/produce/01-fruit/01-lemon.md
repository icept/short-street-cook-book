The workhorse of the citrus corner. We use lemons in almost everything —
dressings, braises, marinades, drinks — and the tree barely pauses across
the year. The juice is sharper in summer; the skin thickens up and the flavour
sweetens noticeably through winter.

## In season

Year-round, but best from June to September. Crop is nearly continuous;
in a good year there are more than you can use.

## Pairs with

- Olive oil — acid and fat, the most reliable partnership in the kitchen
- Sea salt — especially flaked, alongside fresh lemon zest
- Sumac — reinforces the citrus note in dressings and marinades
- Tahini — lemon lifts and cuts the paste's earthiness
- Honey — with preserved lemon rind or zest
- Bay and rosemary — for braises and roasts
- Oily fish — sardines, tuna, salmon; the acid cuts the richness
- Bitter leaves — radicchio, witlof, rocket

## Where to get it locally

- Own tree
- Grower-direct top-ups at **Stirling Farmers Market** (Sundays,
  City of Stirling Administration Building, Cedric Street)

## Notes

**Zest before you juice** — always. Freeze any zest you don't need immediately
in a small zip-lock bag.

**Freeze juice** in ice-cube trays (roughly 2 tbsp per cube) so a glut
doesn't go to waste.

**[Preserved lemons](/recipes/preserves/16-preserved-lemons/):** salt-pack
whole lemons, scored to the base, into a sterilised jar; ready in four weeks,
keep for months in the fridge. Use the rind only — rinse it and scrape out
the pulp before adding.
