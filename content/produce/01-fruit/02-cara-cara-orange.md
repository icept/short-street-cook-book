A navel orange with pink flesh, lower acidity than a standard orange, and a
faint raspberry note underneath the sweetness. We use it from June through to
September and it earns its place in everything from marinades and cocktails to
eating straight off the tree. It is noticeably better than a regular navel
for drinking raw — less sharp, more complex.

## In season

June to September, peaking mid-winter. The fruit develops sweetness slowly;
don't rush harvesting. A fully ripe Cara Cara barely needs squeezing — it
gives juice freely.

## Pairs with

- Cognac — the combination is almost too good; see the roast chicken
- Rosemary — resinous herb against sweet citrus, works in drinks and roasts
- Honey — raw or dark; deepens the berry note in the orange
- Duck and chicken — the sweetness holds its own against rich poultry
- Bitter chocolate — Cara Cara peel candied and dipped is worth the effort
- Pomegranate — complementary sweetness, similar season

## Where to get it locally

- Own tree
- Cara Cara oranges occasionally at **Stirling Farmers Market** (Sundays)
  in season; less common than navels so ask growers directly
- Specialty fruit shops sometimes carry them June–August

## Notes

**Candied peel:** double-blanch the peel to remove bitterness, then simmer in
a light sugar syrup until translucent. Cara Cara peel is excellent here —
the pink tinge holds in the candied rind.

**Juice yield:** expect about 80–100 ml per fruit at peak season. The flesh
colour ranges from pale pink to deep rose depending on how much winter sun the
tree gets.
