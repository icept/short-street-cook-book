---
title: Pasta with Anchovy, Herbs, and Lemon
serves: 4
time: 25 minutes
---

A weeknight staple that tastes more considered than it is. Everything here
either comes from the garden or lives in the pantry. The sort of dish that
makes you glad you kept a tin of anchovies in the cupboard.

## Ingredients

- 400 g spaghetti or linguine
- 8 anchovy fillets in oil, roughly chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, finely sliced
- 1 long red chilli, finely sliced (seeds in or out, your call)
- Zest and juice of 1 lemon
- Large handful flat-leaf parsley, roughly chopped
- 4 tbsp good olive oil, plus extra to finish
- Freshly ground black pepper

## Method

1. Cook the pasta in well-salted boiling water until just al dente. Reserve a
   mug of the pasta water before draining.
2. While the pasta cooks, warm the olive oil in a large, wide pan over
   medium-low heat. Add the anchovy and garlic. Cook gently, stirring, until
   the anchovy dissolves into the oil — about 3 minutes. Don't let the garlic
   colour.
3. Add the chilli and cook for another minute.
4. Drain the pasta and tip it straight into the pan. Add a splash of pasta
   water and toss well over the heat.
5. Off the heat, stir through the lemon zest, lemon juice, and most of
   the parsley.
6. Finish with a drizzle of oil and black pepper. Scatter the remaining
   parsley on top.

## Flavour

Deeply savoury and salty from the anchovy, with brightness from the lemon
cutting through the richness of the oil. The chilli adds a low warmth rather
than heat. It should taste alive and sharp — if it feels flat, add more
lemon juice.

## Why it works

Anchovy dissolved in warm oil becomes the base of the sauce rather than a
topping — you lose the fishiness entirely and gain a deep, savoury backbone
that coats every strand of pasta. The pasta water, starchy from cooking,
emulsifies the oil into something saucy rather than greasy. This is why you
save the pasta water and why you toss the pasta off the heat.

## Substitutions

**Anchovies** — Capers (2 tbsp, rinsed) give a similar briny note, slightly
more acidic. The dish becomes vegetarian but loses some depth; bump up the
olive oil a little to compensate.

**Parsley** — Basil works beautifully in summer. A mix of both is excellent.
Avoid woody herbs here — rosemary or thyme would overwhelm everything else.

**Lemon** — A small splash of white wine vinegar (about 1 tsp) can stand in
if you're out of citrus, but the dish wants the brightness of fresh lemon.

**Chilli** — A pinch of dried chilli flakes is a fine substitute. If you
want no heat at all, leave it out entirely; the dish still holds together.
