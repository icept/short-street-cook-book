---
title: Boxing Day Bisque
category: Soups
serves: 2
time: 2 hours 30 minutes
---

Born of the Australian Christmas tradition of cray and prawns on the table —
this is what to do with the shells the day after. Don't waste the heads or the
leftover meat; the shells make the stock and the meat goes in the bottom of the
bowl. Prawn shells work just as well as cray if that's what you've got.

## Ingredients

### For the bisque

- 4 cray shells (or any leftover prawn shells)
- Cray or prawn meat, reserved for serving
- 6 cloves garlic, quartered
- 1 fennel bulb, quartered
- 2 carrots, sliced 5 mm
- 2 celery sticks, sliced 5 mm
- 1 can diced tomatoes
- 6 large sprigs thyme
- 3 bay leaves
- 3 large shallots
- 375 ml dry vermouth or white wine
- 180 ml cream
- Cayenne pepper, to taste
- Tarragon, to taste
- Salt and pepper, to taste

### For the meat

- 50 g butter
- 2 cloves garlic, crushed and minced

## Method

1. Clean the cray — remove guts and head sacks.
2. Smash the cray shells into small pieces.
3. Heat oil in a French oven and fry the shells for 3–5 minutes.
4. Add the garlic, fennel, carrot, celery, and shallots, and fry for 10 minutes.
5. Deglaze the pan with the vermouth.
6. Add the tomatoes, bay, and thyme, then fill two-thirds with water.
7. Boil on low–medium for 1½ hours, topping up with liquid if it needs it.
8. Strain the liquid into a clean pan. Pick out some of the vegetable matter
   from the solids and add it back to the pan.
9. Season with tarragon, cayenne, salt, and pepper to taste.
10. Heat for 10 minutes to combine.
11. Blitz the soup until smooth, then stir through the cream.
12. In a separate pan, melt the butter.
13. Add the garlic and the reserved meat, and heat gently for 5 minutes.
14. Divide the meat between two bowls and top with the soup to serve.
