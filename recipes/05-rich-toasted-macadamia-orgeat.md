---
title: Rich Toasted Macadamia Orgeat
yield: approx. 2 cups
time: 15 minutes active, plus 12–24 hours steeping
---

A house orgeat made from the macadamia tree in the garden. Toasting the nuts first deepens the flavour considerably — it pushes the syrup past sweet and into something more complex and buttery. Worth making a full batch; it keeps for up to six months in the fridge and improves everything it touches.

## Ingredients

- 270 g raw macadamia nuts
- 400 g Demerara, turbinado, or raw cane sugar
- 250 ml water
- 1 tsp high-quality vanilla extract
- ¼ tsp fine sea salt
- 30 ml high-proof neutral spirit, bourbon, or dark rum

## Method

1. Spread the macadamias on a baking sheet and roast at 135°C for 12–15 minutes until lightly golden. Cool completely, then pulse in a food processor until finely ground — stop before they turn to butter.
2. Combine the sugar and water in a saucepan over medium heat, stirring until the sugar fully dissolves.
3. Add the ground macadamias and salt to the syrup. Simmer gently for 3–4 minutes, then remove from the heat.
4. Cover and steep at room temperature for 12 to 24 hours.
5. Strain through a prepared Chux cloth or cheesecloth, squeezing firmly to extract all oils and liquid.
6. For maximum shelf life, pass the strained syrup a second time through a paper coffee filter without squeezing — this removes microscopic solids that would otherwise shorten its life.
7. Stir in the vanilla and spirit. Bottle in a sterilised glass jar and refrigerate.
